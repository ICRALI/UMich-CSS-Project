Electronics

Electronics shape the way we interact with the world — from the simplest resistor that limits current, to advanced sensors that give life to modern devices. In this space, you'll explore the components that make every circuit possible: LEDs that turn electricity into light, capacitors that store energy like tiny batteries, microcontrollers that act as the “brains” of a system, and much more.

Arduino

Arduino is one of the most popular platforms for learning, experimenting, and building electronic projects. It combines a simple microcontroller board with an intuitive programming environment, allowing anyone — from beginners to engineers — to control sensors, LEDs, motors, and countless other components with just a few lines of code.
